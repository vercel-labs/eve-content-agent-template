# X (Twitter) best practices

A sourced checklist of current tactics for writing on X. Use it as a pre-flight check, not a
recipe — the house voice (first-person, plain, concrete) wins over any single rule.

## The hook

- [ ] The first line earns the stop. Open on the surprising claim, the number, the contradiction,
      or the visceral moment. No throat-clearing, no "A thread on…".
- [ ] Create an open question in the reader's mind — but don't give away the full answer in line one.
- [ ] A hook is not clickbait: it promises only value the post actually delivers. Test it —
      would *you* stop scrolling, and feel satisfied after?
- [ ] Emotional or numeric specificity beats vague inspiration every time.
- [ ] On X the preview shows ~280 characters; if your hook isn't in those first characters,
      most people never tap to read more.

## Length & format

- [ ] One idea per post. Free accounts cap at 280 characters; if you have more, write a thread.
- [ ] Keep posts tight. Sprout finds the 240–259 character band tends to earn the most
      engagement; shorter, punchier posts also travel well. Cut every word that isn't load-bearing.
- [ ] Text-based posts perform well on X — Buffer's analysis found them ahead of video, image,
      and (especially) link-only posts for engagement. Don't add media just to add media.
- [ ] When you do use media, native image or video lifts engagement; X is pushing video-first,
      so prefer native video over a link to YouTube.

## Links

- [ ] External links suppress reach (Sprout cites 50–90% reductions). When a post needs a link,
      put it in the first reply and say so, rather than in the main post.
- [ ] Auto-shortened URLs always count as 23 characters regardless of real length.
- [ ] Space links out — clustering many link posts in a short window lowers click-through per link.

## Hashtags

- [ ] 0–2 real hashtags, max. 1–2 can lift engagement; 3+ tends to drop it. No hashtag walls.

## Engagement & cadence

- [ ] The algorithm rewards fast early engagement and prioritizes replies — open questions and
      genuine conversation starters beat like-bait.
- [ ] No engagement bait ("RT if you agree", "read that again", "let that sink in"). It reads as
      spam and undercuts the voice, whatever old "ask for the retweet" advice says.
- [ ] Consistency beats bursts: a sustainable daily cadence (Buffer suggests ~3–5 posts/day for
      growth-focused accounts) outperforms posting hard for two weeks and burning out.
- [ ] Engagement skews to weekday mornings (Buffer: ~9–11 AM, Tue–Thu strongest), but post when
      *your* audience is active. This is a scheduling note, not a drafting rule.

## Threads

- [ ] The first post is a hook, not a table of contents — it must stand on its own and earn the tap.
- [ ] One beat per post; each should be worth reading even out of context.
- [ ] Add small "expansion hooks" every few posts to keep people scrolling.
- [ ] Number posts (`1/`, `2/`) only when it genuinely aids a longer thread; don't force it.
- [ ] Close with a payoff or one clear takeaway — not "follow me for more".

## X Premium

- Premium accounts can post up to 25,000 characters and get a reach boost, but long posts still
  show only a ~280-character preview in-feed — so the hook discipline above still applies. This is
  context, not a drafting target: default to writing as if the reader is on a free account unless
  told otherwise.
