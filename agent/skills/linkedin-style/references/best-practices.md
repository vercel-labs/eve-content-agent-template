# LinkedIn best practices

A sourced checklist of current tactics for writing on LinkedIn. Use it as a pre-flight check, not
a recipe — the house voice (first-person, plain, concrete) wins over any single rule.

## The hook

- [ ] The opening earns the "…see more" click. Only ~200 characters show on mobile (~300 on
      desktop) before the feed truncates the post — the first line decides whether anyone reads on.
- [ ] Lead with a stop-the-scroll move: a surprising stat, a bold or contrarian claim, an open
      loop (a curiosity gap that only the rest of the post closes), or a one-line mini-story setup.
- [ ] No throat-clearing, no "A few thoughts on…", no "I'm humbled / thrilled to announce".

## Length & format

- [ ] One idea per post. A how-to or a list of concrete takeaways tends to land best.
- [ ] Short lines and single-sentence paragraphs with whitespace between them — it's read on a
      phone, and white space keeps it scannable.
- [ ] Concrete over abstract: a number, a result, a before/after, a specific moment.
- [ ] Deliver the value in the feed. Self-contained text posts are what people most engage with;
      don't make the reader leave to get the point.

## Close

- [ ] End on a memorable line, a single clear takeaway, or one genuine question.
- [ ] A real question invites comments — and comments signal value to the algorithm more than
      likes do. But ask because you want the answer, not as "Agree? 👇" bait.

## Links

- [ ] No external link in the post body. An external link in the post can cut reach noticeably,
      and link-in-comments posts have been measured at roughly 2–3x the impressions of
      link-in-post ones.
- [ ] If the piece needs a link, put it in the first comment and say "link in the comments".

## Hashtags

- [ ] 3–5 relevant hashtags, no more. Precision beats volume.
- [ ] Prefer specific/niche tags (`#BusinessWriting`) over broad ones (`#Business`).
- [ ] Group them at the end of the post to keep the message clean. Hashtags now act as topic/SEO
      signals, not a discovery feed — they amplify strong copy, they don't rescue weak copy.
- [ ] CamelCase multi-word tags (`#SocialMediaMarketing`) for readability and screen readers.

## Mentions & format

- [ ] @mention only people likely to respond; keep it to a handful per post.
- [ ] At most one emoji, and only if it earns its place. No emoji clutter, no engagement-bait
      reaction polls or "like if…" chains — the algorithm suppresses those.
- [ ] Vary format over time. Document/carousel posts (uploaded PDFs) and short video tend to draw
      stronger engagement than plain text, but the best format is the one you can sustain with
      genuinely useful content. See `post-specs.md` for carousel specs.
